DANIEL MINUS DEPRESSION - DANIEL CAESARS CASE STUDY 01

The contagious cadence in Caesars new release Case Study 01, comes as no surprise to his listeners. What does surprise us however, is his visual (or rather audible) amount of growth... Case Study 01 introduces us to a new symphony of eclectic vibrations. Daniel quite literally transports us to a new world with the introduction of Entropy. Greeting us galactically, Daniel fully launches us into a universe of jazzy electric vibrations. Case Study 01 distinguishes itself from Daniels other albums with its high toned, good vibed, smoothly rhythmic tracks such as “Cyanide” which is reminiscent of a summer day spent with your boo as you both cruise down the highway gazing at a vibrant summer sunset. Right when things seem to hit peak flow, Daniel seamlessly transitions into “LOVE AGAIN” where we’re greeted with a Brandy and Caesar rnb throwback to the past. Let the 90’s baby making begin. The real slap to the ear comes when were greeted with Frontal Lobe Muzik. Singing in a uniquely unexpected futuristic autotune that takes his listeners for a sensational launch into a new realm, reminiscent of Kanyes 808’s and Heartbreaks use of autotune, Daniel Caesar utilizes the electric autotune with his own fingerprint. I think one of the best parts of Daniel Caesars new album is that each song is uniquely original to the listeners ear. While always providing his signature Daniel Cesar vibe to each song. Daniel Caesar has clearly undergone a great stage of growth and evolution since his last releases of Pilgrim Paradise and Freudian which while tasty to the ear failed to provide unique energy to each track. It’s clear from Case Study 01 , that Daniel Caesar is far from being just a sad, romantic, Mr. Lonely boy. It’s as if Daniel Caesar has acquired a new found energy source, a new reason for living . He accepts things as they are and with acceptance comes freedom. Freedom from judgment of failure, freedom to experiment with sounds, freedom to embrace new melodies and energies. A remarkable aspect of Daniels new album is that he doesn’t simply abandon his previous sound, he evolves with it. Songs like “SUPERPOSITON” are reminiscent of the Caesar we used to know. Yet the majority of his songs are like a chartered path of growth. One can only hope Caesar continues to grow and evolve to infinity and beyond.
